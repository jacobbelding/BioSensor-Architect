Genetic Elements

RD29A Promoter

Arabidopsis thaliana • AT5G52310 upstream region
  • Size: 1.5 kb upstream sequence from TSS
  • Cis-elements: DRE/CRT boxes, ABRE motifs
  • Regulation: ABA-responsive, cold/drought-inducible
  • Background: Originally from RD29A (responsive to desiccation)
Key reference: Yamaguchi-Shinozaki et al. Plant Cell (1994); Recent validation: PMID: 34053006

Aequorea victoria • Reporter gene
  • Size: 714 bp coding sequence

Literature Validation

Supporting Evidence & Design Rationale

RD29A promoter validation in transgenic systems The RD29A promoter has been extensively characterized for stress-responsive expression. Recent work (PMID: 34053006) demonstrated that the RD29A promoter constitutively drives stress-inducible expression in transgenic citrus systems, confirming its utility for biosensor applications. The promoter contains well-characterized DRE/CRT and ABRE elements that respond to DREB1A/CBF and ABA-responsive transcription factors respectively.
Gateway cloning system compatibility The pGWB5 binary vector provides a reliable framework for plant transformation with kanamycin selection. This system has been widely used for Arabidopsis transformation and provides consistent transgene expression. The vector backbone includes appropriate T-DNA borders for Agrobacterium-mediated transformation.
GFP maturation kinetics consideration GFP maturation requires approximately 30 minutes at 22°C, meaning early time points (1-3 hours) may underestimate true promoter activity. The characterization protocol accounts for this by focusing on time points ≥6 hours for quantitative measurements, with earlier time points serving as trend indicators only.
Baseline expression and sensitivity Stress-responsive promoters typically show 10-20% baseline expression under non-stress conditions due to leaky transcription. This background provides a detection threshold but may complicate detection of mild stress. Expected dynamic range of 5-10 fold based on RD29A qRT-PCR studies in literature.
Transgene silencing risk Plant transgenes, particularly those with repetitive elements, are susceptible to epigenetic silencing over multiple generations. Mitigation strategies include: (1) selection of multiple independent T2 lines, (2) maintenance through T3 generation to assess stability, and (3) potential incorporation of matrix attachment regions if silencing is observed.

Measurement Protocol

  • Time points: 6h, 12h, 24h, 48h, 72h post-treatment
  • Detection: Plate reader (Ex 485/Em 535 nm) or confocal microscopy
  • Replicates: N=6 independent T2 lines, 3 seedlings per line
  • Statistics: Two-way ANOVA (treatment × time) with Tukey's post-hoc
  • Controls: p35S::GFP (positive), promoterless GFP (negative), Col-0 (autofluorescence)
