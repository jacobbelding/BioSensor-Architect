New Component Specifications (v2 Additions)

SlAMT1.1 Promoter

Cassette B · NH₄⁺ sensor
  • Drives expression of the CRISPRi machinery specifically under ammonium excess conditions
  • Alternative: SlAMT1.2 promoter, or promoter of NADH-GOGAT (nitrogen metabolism gene strongly upregulated by NH₄⁺ excess)
  • Activates dCas9-repressor only when NH₄⁺ is high, leaving Cassette A uninhibited under normal K⁺ starvation

dCas9-SRDX (CRISPRi Repressor)

Cassette B · AND NOT executor
  • Nuclease-dead Cas9 fused to a transcriptional repressor domain (e.g. SRDX — EAR-repression motif)
  • Guided by sgRNA from Cassette B2 to physically bind SKRP or SlANT1 CDS on Cassette A
  • Sterically blocks RNA Pol II without DNA cleavage — fully reversible when NH₄⁺ drops
  • sgRNA expressed from a Pol III promoter (SlU6) for efficient, constitutive guide production

SlSOS1pTS-21 Promoter

Cassette C · Na⁺ / salinity sensor
  • Specific allele of the SlSOS1 (Salt Overly Sensitive 1) gene promoter
  • Contains a functionally validated CRT/DRE core cis-element bound by the salt-inducible transcription factor SlDREB2
  • Drives massive upregulation strictly in response to high salinity — not activated by K⁺ deficiency alone
  • Bypasses the SKRP entirely, ensuring K⁺ starvation is reported even when salt stress suppresses SlHAK5 expression

SlANT1 CDS (Cassette C copy)

Cassette C · independent reporter
  • Identical CDS to Cassette A (Solyc10g086260) — same anthocyanin output, different driver
  • Operates completely independently of Cassette A and the CRISPRi machinery of Cassette B
  • The two SlANT1 copies (A and C) can additively amplify the purple pigmentation signal under combined K⁺ + Na⁺ stress

Design Notes & Considerations

CRISPRi sgRNA target selection The sgRNA in Cassette B2 should be designed to target a site within the SKRP itself (ideally near the TSS or a critical GCC-box) or the 5′ end of the SlANT1 CDS in Cassette A. Targeting the SKRP is preferable as it creates a complete transcriptional block. Target the non-template strand for best repression efficiency. Avoid designing the guide against regions shared with Cassette C to prevent off-target silencing of the Na⁺ bypass.
Potential pitfall: Cassette B does not suppress Cassette C The sgRNA in Cassette B targets Cassette A only. Cassette C uses the entirely unrelated SlSOS1pTS-21 promoter and a separate SlANT1 copy, so it is immune to the CRISPRi brake. This is by design (the OR gate must fire independently), but note that under conditions of both NH₄⁺ high AND Na⁺ high, the Na⁺ bypass will still produce purple pigmentation — this is the intended behavior (see truth table row 7). Confirm this is the desired phenotype for your experimental conditions.
dCas9 leaky expression risk Even low basal expression of dCas9-SRDX could partially suppress Cassette A. Choose a tight NH₄⁺-specific promoter with minimal basal activity. SlAMT1.1 is preferred over constitutive alternatives. Validate basal repressor expression levels under K⁺-starvation conditions with no NH₄⁺ before interpreting reporter output.
Vector loading and size considerations Three cassettes significantly increase total T-DNA insert size. Estimate: SKRP (~2.2 kb) + SlANT1 CDS + SlHSP_term + SlAMT1.1_prom + dCas9 (~4.1 kb) + SlU6_prom + sgRNA + SlSOS1pTS-21_prom + SlANT1 CDS + SlHSP_term ≈ ~15–18 kb total insert. Confirm your binary vector can accommodate this payload. For transient assays, pEAQ or ternary (Tv) systems are still recommended to maximize per-cell expression. Alternatively, consider delivering Cassettes A and C in one vector and Cassette B (the CRISPRi module) in a co-infiltrated second vector, which reduces per-vector payload and allows modular testing.
Additive signal under combined stress Both Cassettes A and C drive SlANT1 expression independently. Under genuine combined K⁺ + Na⁺ stress (with no high NH₄⁺), two separate pools of SlANT1 protein will accumulate, potentially giving a stronger or earlier pigmentation signal. This additive effect should be calibrated against single-stress controls.
